# 🏚️ # TryHackMe – The House That Watches Back Walkthrough

> A story-driven Capture The Flag (CTF) challenge focused on digital forensics, cryptography, steganography, reverse engineering, and logical analysis.

---

## 📖 Story

On the outskirts of a forgotten city, there stands a house no one dares to enter.

A boy goes missing after entering it.  
A witness reports it… and then disappears.

A sheriff and his team investigate.

But inside the house…

They get separated.  
They get trapped.  
And something feels wrong.

As you progress deeper, you begin to uncover a disturbing truth:

> **The house was never haunted.**

---

## 🎯 Objective

Play through 5 connected challenges and uncover the final truth behind the haunted house.

Each challenge reveals a piece of the story.

**Flag Format:**  

THM{...}


---

## 🧩 Challenges Overview

| # | Title      | Category        | Difficulty | Description |
|--|-----------|----------------|------------|------------|
| 1 | Evidence  | Forensics       | Medium     | Analyze a suspicious image and uncover hidden data inside it. |
| 2 | Radio     | Cryptography    | Medium     | Decode a distorted transmission and recover hidden content. |
| 3 | Hallway   | Steganography   | Medium     | Find hidden data inside an image using stego techniques. |
| 4 | Basement  | Reverse Engineering | Hard  | Analyze a binary to uncover hidden logic and the real flag. |
| 5 | System    | Logic / OSINT   | Hard       | Identify patterns in logs and decode the final truth. |

---

## 🛠️ Skills & Tools Required

This CTF tests real-world cybersecurity skills:

- 🔍 `exiftool` — Metadata analysis  
- 🧱 `binwalk` — File carving  
- 🔐 `base64` — Encoding/Decoding  
- 🖼️ `steghide` — Steganography  
- 🧠 `strings` — Binary inspection  
- 📦 `zip/unzip` — Archive handling  
- 🧩 Logical thinking & pattern recognition

## 🧠 Key Concepts Covered

- Hidden data in files (file injection)
- Fake flags & misdirection
- Multi-layer encoding
- Password reuse across challenges
- Steganography & file type spoofing
- Reverse engineering basics
- Story-driven challenge design
